Other Projects

The Double Description Method (Polyhedral Sets)

Polyhedral sets are used in optimizing compilers to model, analyze, transform and schedule for-loops of computer programs. An important operation on polyhedral sets is the conversion between H-representation (a system of linear inequalities) and V-representation (vertices and rays). This conversion can be done by the Double Description Method. I implemented the Double Description Method in C++ which will be integrated in the Maple software.

Supervisor: Dr. Marc Moreno Maza

Energy Efficient Broadcasting Algorithms for Wireless Networks

Energy efficiency is crucial for wireless ad hoc networks as they rely on batteries with a finite lifespan. Two energy-efficient broadcasting algorithms, namely the Broadcast Incremental Power (BIP) and the Embedded Wireless Multicast Advantage (EWMA), along with their complexity analyses are reviewed in this report. Also, the BIP algorithm is implemented in C++ with successful tests for small and large network sizes.
